Exam/Quiz Details and Dates

CMPT 295

  • The first quiz will Wednesday June 24 in the lecture time (13:30-14:20) and in the lecture room (WMC3520). It will be 50 minutes, on paper. Bring your SFU student card or other ID. No cheat sheets, electronics, etc may be used. Of course, it's individual work and you may not communicate with others during the quiz. The quiz will cover material up to and including the "Limits and Overflow" slides in the "Binary Representations" slide deck. I will expect you to have done lab exercises 1-5. There will be some multiple choice questions, and a few longer-answer questions. There will certainly be questions of the form "write x86-64 assembly code that...". You are expected to be able to write assembly on paper that's close enough to correct syntax to fool the TAs when they're marking it by eye. This cheatsheet will be included as the last page of the quiz: https://ggbaker.ca/tmp/quiz1-cheatsheet.pdf I included some powers of 2: you won't have a calculator (and don't think there's much value in testing your mental arithmetic) so any integer conversions can be left in the form "128+32+4". I omitted the <64-bit versions of the registers to save space. If there is a need for the 32-, 16-, or 8-bit registers, I will say in the question text something like "%dil is the 8-bit version of %rdi" as appropriate.
